Biscoff Cookie Butter Macarons

Ingredients:

For the macarons:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 large egg white
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon food coloring (optional)
  • 1/4 cup Biscoff cookie butter

For the filling:

  • 1/4 cup Biscoff cookie butter
  • 1 tablespoon softened butter

Instructions:

  1. Make the macaron shells:
    • Sift the almond flour and powdered sugar together.
    • In a clean bowl, beat the egg white and cream of tartar until foamy. Gradually add the granulated sugar, beating until stiff peaks form.
    • Gently fold the dry ingredients into the egg white mixture until just combined.
    • Fold in the Biscoff cookie butter and food coloring, if using.
    • Pipe the batter onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Let the shells rest for 30 minutes.
    • Bake in a preheated oven at 300°F (150°C) for 15-20 minutes, or until the shells are set.
    • Let the shells cool completely before assembling.
  2. Make the filling:
    • In a small bowl, combine the Biscoff cookie butter and
    • softened butter. Beat until smooth.
  3. Assemble the macarons:
    • Pipe the filling onto one of the macaron shells. Sandwich with the other shell.
    • Let the macarons rest for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Tips:

  • Make sure your equipment is clean and dry when making macarons, as any moisture can affect the results.
  • If the shells are too sticky, let them rest for longer before piping.
  • Store the macarons in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days.
  • You can adjust the amount of food coloring to achieve your desired shade.

Galaxy Macarons

Ingredients:

For the macarons:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 large egg white
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar

  • 1/4 cup blueberry puree

    • Sift the almond flour and powdered sugar together.
    • In a clean bowl, beat the egg white and cream of tartar until foamy. Gradually add the granulated sugar, beating until stiff peaks form.

    • Let the shells cool completely before assembling.
  2. Make the filling:
    • In a small bowl, combine the blueberry puree and softened butter. Beat until smooth.
  3. Assemble the macarons:
    • Pipe the filling onto one of the macaron shells. Sandwich with the other shell.
    • Let the macarons rest for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Tips:

  • Make sure your equipment is clean and dry when making macarons, as any moisture can affect the results.
  • If the shells are too sticky, let them rest for longer before piping.
  • You can experiment with different color combinations to create your own unique galaxy effect.
  • Store the macarons in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days.

Strawberry Macaron Shells

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 large egg white
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon food coloring (pink or red)
  • 1/4 cup strawberry puree

Instructions:

  1. Sift the dry ingredients: Sift the almond flour and powdered sugar together.
  2. Whip the egg whites: In a clean bowl, beat the egg white and cream of tartar until foamy. Gradually add the granulated sugar, beating until stiff peaks form.
  3. Combine the wet and dry ingredients: Gently fold the dry ingredients into the egg white mixture until just combined.
  4. Add the strawberry puree: Fold in the strawberry puree and food coloring.
  5. Pipe the batter: Pipe the batter onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Let the shells rest for 30 minutes.
  6. Bake the shells: Bake in a preheated oven at 300°F (150°C) for 15-20 minutes, or until the shells are set.
  7. Cool the shells: Let the shells cool completely before assembling.

Tips:

  • Make sure your equipment is clean and dry when making macarons, as any moisture can affect the results.
  • If the shells are too sticky, let them rest for longer before piping.
  • You can adjust the amount of food coloring to achieve your desired shade.
  • Store the macaron shells in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days.
These strawberry macaron shells are ready to be filled with your favorite filling! You can use a classic buttercream, a fruity jam, or even a chocolate ganache.
